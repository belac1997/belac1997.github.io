Security With Human Centered Design

When it comes to computer security, most people think about firewalls and complicated programs that keep your computer safe from viruses. However, humans are one of the biggest weaknesses when it comes to security. Most people do not know all the interworking’s of a computer system and will take shortcuts when a system seems to complex to deal with. When a security breach happens due to a person’s mistake, most programmers will simply say it was human error and that people need to be taught how to use their complicated programs.

However, human center design aims to solve this problem by building security with people being the focus. There are three big ways human centered design has changed security.

  • Human Interaction
  • Training
  • Reduction in Frustartion

Computers practically speak a different language compared to humans. People sometimes have no idea what the computer is saying to them. By creating a system which accounts for human interaction first, you can

  • Enable people to understand what the computer is telling them
  • Cause less confusion and frustration
  • Have a more positive experience with the computer

Have a professional teach your employees on how to use a new fancy program. People relate to people better than a ReadMe.txt document. By having a professional physically teach people how use their program, this allows for the learners to

  • Be engaged in the learning process
  • See how a professional interacts with the program
  • Ask questions immediately if they find something confusing

The last thing you want to build a system that is frustrating to work with. Even the smallest of inconveniences can cause people’s attitudes to change. This is especially true with technology since it was designed to be used by people. A reduction in frustration will

  • Reduce people neglecting to use the security software
  • Increase productivity
  • Promote a heathier work environment
